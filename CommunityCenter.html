The Truth about TikTok

By Raghav Sriram
July 4th, 2020
Trends

During this quarantine, it is easy to spend hours scrolling through TikTok watching short videos of people dancing to famous songs. Personally, in many group chats I’ve been to, many people would share TikToks and react to them. I guess I missed out on the TikTok craze. I’ve never been really interested in downloading the app. I don’t see the point in watching others dance and make short but relatable comedy sketches, but I guess others do. But one thing I’ve noticed is that all the people who use TikTok always seem to be addicted to it. So what makes it so addicting?...

The premise is simple and is in its name, tik tok is the sound a clock makes giving the implication to users that they can pass time by using this app. Most people who download this app have honorable intentions. They download it to pass time and connect with others, but as they keep on using the app it becomes harder and harder to leave. This situation is incredibly similar to other social media, Snapchat. The more you use Snapchat and TikTok the more you will get addicted and the more time you’ll spend on the app. Similar to narcotics when you first use TikTok it takes a few minutes to get satisfied, but as you keep on using it that satisfaction takes longer to achieve until eventually, it won't bring any happiness at all and just be a waste of time. With social media like TikTok, the harms always seem to outweigh the benefits. In the case of Tiktok it becomes addictive, eventually, a waste of time, makes you feel lonely, increases emotional instability, and does not have any educational benefit.

With that said if you use TikTok responsibly and correctly, it is a valuable resource that can help you meet new people and make everlasting connections through the short videos posted. I hope that after reading this you don’t become addicted to TikTok like some people at NexEdge Innovations :).

Injustice

By Raghav Sriram
July 4th, 2020
Current Issues

Systematic racism within the black community has been ongoing for the past centuries. Many believe Africans first faced racism when they were brought over as slaves to the Americas years ago, but that is hardly the case. Pottery found in Ancient Greece, over 2,500 years ago, displays grotesque and plain racist imagery. Since ancient times those of African ethnicity have never been given the benefit of the doubt, from the second they are born people see them as a threat to their livelihood, an underlying issue in our society that must be fixed....

But let me also paint another story, one that seems completely opposite yet oddly similar. I’d like you to imagine being a child born to Asian parents. Your parents are strict but not as strict as the societal expectations placed upon you. From the minute you are born people make assumptions on your character and what you’re going to be in the future. You are expected to be a doctor or a scientist and nothing else is a disappointment. Society expects you to do well in school, compete in multiple competitions, and pursue multiple extracurricular activities. If you decide to disobey these strict guidelines you are ridiculed, teased, and become an exception to what society envisions you to be. This does not occur in isolated areas, in fact, many Asians in Carmel feel this pressure too. An article I wrote for the HiLite in the March publication told the stories of the many stereotypes Asian Americans at Carmel High school have faced.

In the United States, Asian Americans are perceived as a model minority whose behaviors and actions other ethnicities should follow. While these stereotypes are largely positive, contrary to the stereotypes African Americans face, those who do not live up to them are seen as failures. While I’m not trying to compare the injustices African Americans face every day with those of Asian Americans face, I would like to point out that while the stereotypes Asians face largely differ from those of African Americans face, both groups face disastrous effects from these perceptions whether it's a police shooting an innocent black man or an Asian student committing suicide after facing tremendous pressure. Change MUST be made, it’s the only way we will be able to move forward as a modern society. We MUST educate those who are ignorant, we can’t let our biases and prejudices blur our vision and affect the perceptions we have on others. We have initiated the change but we MUST keep on pushing, we can’t stop until we all win collectively as a nation and as a society.

Why the 2019-20 NBA Season Should Come Back

By Eamon Mukhopadhyay
July 4th, 2020
Sports

On March 11th, 2020, the NBA announced the suspension of the season from the CoVid-19 after a Utah Jazz player was diagnosed with Covid-19. Soon after, many players were diagnosed with the virus, which had not been fatal to any player but remained a risk to the people around them. But, officially announced on June 4th, a plan to continue the season starting July 31st was approved by the NBA. Of course, many fans across the globe were extremely excited about the opportunity to see the world’s largest professional basketball league finally continuing, after months of discussion by the league and its players. However, some individual players and even fans object to the change, arguing the virus’s dangers as well as the George Floyd protests would distract the world from its real issues. However, I personally believe that continuing the league is necessary for the league, and here is why....

First off, the NBA would suffer tremendous financial loss if the season did not re-open this season. Because national broadcasting for the NBA will most likely be at an all-time high (if not, around the top in history), the league will use the opportunity to gain a massive amount of financial gain. If the season did not reopen, it is possible that the league would not have the funds to reopen the 2020-21 season, and that season would be canceled altogether, putting a few players’ careers in jeopardy (some will get older, and their skill-sets will decline). To put it into perspective, the NBA is projected to lose $500 million to 1 billion if officially canceled; with the season re-opening, it will prevent these massive losses which could affect the next season as well.

Second off, the sport of basketball brings everyone together, and it has for many years. The NBA is a very diverse sport, incorporating players from all over the world. It introduces many cultures and different styles of playing the game. The fans also are brought together through their fan bases, and mainly peaceful rivalries between fan bases brings fun competition to the game. The reopening of the NBA season will contribute to the overall spirit of basketball.

Finally, the player’s in the sport will be isolated significantly. There are many set hotels and significant guidelines for players during this time. The players and league officials will be isolated at the Disney Springs Sports Complex in Orlando, Florida, isolated by themselves with their families. The NBA has planned out a tremendous amount of policies between different teams to prevent another Covid-19 shut down, such as referees having to wear masks, and extremely strict testing protocols, as well as the ability for players to be excused (not forced by the league to play, payments are decided by the team).

Bringing people together is what sports can do very well, and the NBA will be able to do that if they reopen the season, saving their own financial situation, while still establishing isolation guidelines to stop CoVid-19 spread. While there are some reasonable Covid-19 scares if the season continues, I strongly believe that re-opening the season will help the league in the long run.

Why the College Football Playoff Needs to Change

By Sam Tan
August 28th, 2020
Sports

College football is very popular across the nation to many people. The exciting games, the major upsets, and the electrifying atmosphere are something else. However, I see one problem with the playoff system. A biased committee decides on just 4 teams to compete for a national title. There are many reasons to add teams or extend them, and I have some good reasons....

The first one is it would bring in more profit from more national TV coverage and attendance (during normal times). More games with more teams simply means more money and hype for the sport. Schools will also receive good incentives as well for competing in a big game.

The next one is it allows for more teams to at least have a chance to compete for a title. For example, in 2017 and 2018, UCF went undefeated in the regular season. They weren’t the best team in the nation but still deserved to at least have a shot at glory. Even more unfair is the fact that there are 5 big conferences, 5 middle to smaller ones, and independents to compete in 4 meager spots. Again, it’s not about being the highest ranked but more so about teams getting a chance to join the action because they deserve an attempt.

Finally, the committee that ranks teams is biased in some ways. Long time or historical programs that weren’t the best in one season might be ranked higher for their brand name. Also, teams from the power 5 conferences are seen as superior just for being in a their conference sometimes. With more teams representing more of college football as a whole the biases would be less prevalent. This to me is a clear solution to fix this great college sport.

Despite the new map and graphics, which were worse in my opinion, most of the items were the same, therefore, the game felt no more fun than before. The new bots that were added to ease up the game were useless, and made players feel too confident. So confident to the point where when they met a real player they had to know the idea they were fighting one.

In conclusion, Fortnite, for me, was less of a roller-coaster and more of a single up and down experience. There was a time of excitement, and from there, it only went down, and never recovered. This is the downfall of Fortnite.

Advice to Future Highschoolers

By Nicholas Jagelka
July 4th, 2020
School

Although high school is some of the most stressful and exciting years of your life, there are a few tips to ensure the best path to success. There are so many ways to improve one’s method of working, such as being as efficient with your time as possible and not procrastinating. The classes that high school offers give you a sense of freedom, but it’s important to be able to achieve success taking those classes. A good area to start ... could be taking honors classes to gain a better comprehension of difficult class material and study tips and tricks. It’s crucial to take as many effective notes as possible and to continuously read and understand them. AP classes are also a good idea early on as a way to understand the structure of college classes.

Now that we have classes out of the way, we can talk about how high school prioritizes freedom for the student. When your day begins, you must be always sitting at your seat when the bell rings. Every passing period, you have time to talk to friends, grab a snack from a vending machine, and even get coffee from the student-run café. Lunch is divided into four different times. Your lunch schedule is organized based on what your third class on blue and gold days are. For example, I had a Biology class during my third class on blue days. Therefore, I was admitted to Lunch A on blue days. Gold days I had an Orchestral class. Therefore, I was admitted to Lunch D. After lunch, you simply follow the rest of your class schedule until the end of your day. When it is time to go, there are various ways to leave the school including the bus, or carpool. Since high school is so close to the library, it can be beneficial to study after school with some friends, to better comprehend a concept for a certain class. And that's pretty much the schedule for an ordinary highschool day. Being a freshman is nerve-racking, I know I was nervous too. But as you go on throughout the year you become more comfortable and maybe some of these tips and tricks could help you get there. Good luck! You got this.
