Carmel Clay Schools NEEDS Metal Detectors

By Sophia Yang
July 4th, 2020

Carmel is a great city, however, there are some specific things I personally would change, regarding school safety. We spend so much taxpayer money constructing statues on our roundabouts, yet not a single Carmel Clay public school has metal detectors. Although we have police within our schools, I think it’s important to install these. With this, we can eliminate shooters coming into the school armed (so this is a preventative measure for school shootings)....

Although our police inside our schools are well trained and armed, if a school shooter were to open fire, there would still most likely be a number of students or staff harmed or even killed before the police are alerted. Many students, including myself, are wary of these events occurring, and it can be detrimental to a healthy school environment.

The benefits of implementing metal detectors inside our school would greatly outweigh the cons such as cost and the metal detectors setting off false alarms. Schools such as Sycamore have already implemented these within their buildings. These would greatly improve the safety of our Carmel Clay schools and would be relieving for both the students, staff and families of Carmel

Carmel Should Implement More Security Cameras

By Eric Guo
July 4th, 2020

Carmel is recognized as one of the safest cities in America, however that does not mean Carmel is perfect. There are still counts of vandalism or theft that sometimes go unsolved or take too long to be dealt with. These crimes can happen in neighborhoods or in popular areas such as downtown Carmel. Many say improving police could solve this problem, but the problem is police can not watch every move 24/7. Therefore we need a more efficient solution to this problem. That solution being security cameras....

Cameras are effective because they are inexpensive, require low maintenance, and can be automated effortlessly. Also, more neighborhoods also need to be equipped with security cameras to prevent property damage or vandalism. Sometimes vandalism or even accidental damage, such as a kid riding his bike and denting the neighbor’s car, go unsolved since there is no way to find out who committed the act. However, with security cameras, this will not only improve security but also help make these accidents be dealt with smoothly.

Overall, there is almost no downside to adding more security cameras to Carmel, since they require relatively low funding, and bring an impact to Carmel and its citizens. Together we can make Carmel safer for everyone.

Is It a Good Idea to Go to School Next Month?

By Dev Mathur
July 4th, 2020

I personally feel that school shouldn’t open up on August 11th. I know that the Carmel school board is not only wanting to reopen the schools in our district because of the teachers jobs and their wages, but also because they are wanting to follow what the governor orders, which will most likely be that the schools will reopen in the fall. Personally, for me, there are pros and cons of going back to school, and the pros of virtual school outweigh the cons of it for me....

I got to manage my time better and I got to spend more time with my parents and to do toner things I didn’t have time to do during the school year. But for safety reasons, I feel that school shouldn’t open on the 11th for two reasons. First, how will we social distance? There are over 5,000 students that attend Carmel High School and it will be very difficult to keep that in place. Plus, the initiative of wearing masks isn’t the most practical because it cannot be supplied properly to every student. Secondly, once the flu arrives, I feel it will become very unsafe for students to go to school because it will be hard for it not to spread throughout the environment of the school. I also feel that if students stay at home, they will be staying until most likely January because of flu season.

Soccer Should Be a Sport in Middle Schools

By Avinash Valuveri
July 4th, 2020

Middle schools should include soccer as a sport in their schools. Soccer is by far the world’s most popular sport and should be a sport at middle schools. Since Middle Schoolers have no way to play soccer for free, they are forced to pay money to get good enough at the sport to play it at the high school. This means that poorer families will never get an opportunity to play this sport.... Many people play travel soccer during middle school, if there is a free version, then many families can save expenses and encourage their children to play soccer at school rather than at a soccer club. This can also raise awareness for this sport, which has been largely unpopular in the US compared to other sports. This can give students a better understanding of the culture in other countries and bring more connectivity to other countries.
